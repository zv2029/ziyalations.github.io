ACQUAPOLIS


Welcome to Acquapolis, an immersive and interactive installation, current population? Fish (so far). It brings to life a small-scale, endearing aquarium via projection mapping. This is inspired by my interest in the natural world, particularly my desire to constantly be in a natural ambience.

About Acquapolis

Acquapolis came to fruition during a study session. In the background, I tend to play music and visual ambience, often mimicking an underwater world, because I find corals and fishes fluttering past mesmerising. As a result, I thought to create my own underwater world one could interact with.

Projection Mapping

To realise the projection mapping, I used MadMapper. I wanted to find a way for the audience to interact with the creatures themselves, so I chose to project these videos into fishbowls. Using Spout, I managed to live capture my Unity visuals right into MadMapper.

3d_rotation
3D Development

For my software, I have used Unity for the foundation of my visuals. From the Unity Asset store, I plucked a number of low-poly aquatic assets, including the decoration of the environment and creatures. I used Navigation AI for the fishes in the bowls, adding hidden waypoints inside, whilst also animating some movements myself.

developer_board
Arduino

The interactivity aspect takes place with the hardware. Using Arduino Firmata and touch sensors, I bring the spice of interactivity to the aquarium. Each sensor has a role, I connected each analogue pin to a desired outcome. The touch sensors are placed on the bowl, and for example, touching the bowl with the goldfish will trigger the animation of a ripple.
